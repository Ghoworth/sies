Asset Choice and Value




Selecting the Appropriate Assets



Choosing which assets to incorporate into a VPP system could be as simple as using the assets currently available to particular owners. Where assets are to be sourced in the future, the judicious selection of assets will have an impact on financial performance and becomes more of an involved process. Essentially the VPP developer needs to calculate the benefit to them of operating a VPP. That is do the benefits or revenues outweighh the costs. In particular VPP developers should consider:

  • Location of assets
  • Grid conditions around assets location (Need for flexibility and limits on export/imports)
  • Markets Available (and prices which drives potential revenues)
  • Asset characteristics e.g. Wind output, variability of the output.
Using third party VPP solutions will incur costs, depending on the commercial arrangements. Additional fees may be required for specific modules (Machine learning, Weather forecasting etc). Upfront fees for third party software and subsequent "maintenance or use fees" will determine the minimum level of revenues (the "break-even revenue") and assets required. Similarly for home-grown solutions there will be costs associated with access to markets and for services such as weather forecasts as well as for the development of the system. The addition of new assets will result in the addition of new capex and opex, which may or may not be remunerated by the use of VPP.

Using the Spreadsheet Calculator


The embedded spreadsheet shown below, can be used to calculate yearly profits, for a set of assets providing X MW at a certain utilisation. Onshore Wind assets typically have utilisations of around 30% whilst Solar PV only around 10%. You can download for use offline. Note some of the yellow cells have formulas in and are driven off some of the other inputs. You are welcome to overwrite these. Note No carbon benefits have been shown.

Annualized Capital Costs/Charge (ACC)
Capital costs have been represented as Annualized Capital charges in the yearly profit statements. They have been calculated using the number of years and an appropriate discount rate.

Flexibility Market Assumptions
The markets are represented as a simple availability and utilisation charge and based on the current markets for DSO's/DNO's e.g. via Piclo. DSO's/DNO's would be looking for flexibility for a number of hours during the year at specific locations. They would typically pay an availability fee £/MW/h for the hours on standby whether dispatched or not and a utilisation fee for actual energy dispatched or reduced £/MWh. Users can input this data. Some typical values have been input as a starting point but this will be location specific. Note the spreadsheet assumes that volumes are put aside for DSO/DNO flex they would not be able to be used in other markets. Other Specific markets might allow for flexibility to be sold in to multiple markets.
